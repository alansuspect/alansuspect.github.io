Shop

The devotion Hong Kong applies to shopping is a sight in its own right. The city's heritage as an international centre of trade has led to an incredible variety of goods, while the local passion for buying and selling infects almost every corner of the city - and all those who enter it.

Shopping Areas

Pound the shopping pavement one neighbourhood at a time.

  • Mong Kok

    Everything imaginable is bought and sold in this blur of neon signs and 'peoplescapes'

  • Admiralty, Central and SoHo

    Where the rich, the famous and the star-struck flash their plastic

  • Causeway Bay

    For the big guns: some call this the Olympic Games of shopping

  • Stanley

    Shop at a popular market in a laid-back seaside village

Street Markets and Shopping Streets

If it can be bought, it can likely be found at a shopping street or market here.

  • Bird Garden

    A popular gathering place for songbird enthusiasts

  • Goldfish Market

    Discover every colour and shade of luck-bringing goldfish

  • Ladies' Market

    About a kilometre of street-market bustle and clothing, accessories and souvenir bargains

  • Temple Street Night Market

    Souvenirs, snacks, opera singers and fortune tellers – ordered chaos in action.

Malls and Department Stores

Hong Kong's modern monuments to shopping

  • Fashion Walk

    A street complex of high-fashion and Japanese and European brands

  • Pacific Place

    A swanky mall of high-end men’s and women’s fashion

  • ifc mall

    One of Hong Kong’s most visually appealing shopping destinations

  • 1881 Heritage

    Shop and dine at the former Marine Police Headquarters
