Attractions

Asia’s world city punches well above its weight class when it comes to things to keep a visitor out of trouble. We’ve already done the groundwork by pulling together the most popular attractions, the best themed amusements, and all the top ways to enjoy the city’s centrepiece - Victoria Harbour.

The Peak

Out-this-world view of skyscrapers, the glittering harbour and the green hills of the distant New Territories. The highest point on Hong Kong Island, this has been the city's most exclusive neighbourhood since colonial times.

Hong Kong Disneyland

Magic, adventure and the world's favourite cast of characters await. Embarjk on a journey with magical adventures for all ages!

Ladies' Market

With over 100 stalls of bargain clothing, accessories and souveniers, the Ladies' Market on Tung Choi Street provides a one-kilometre stretch on which to practice your haggling skills. It gets its name from the huge amount of clothing and accessories on sale for women of all ages.

Golden Bauhinia Square (and HKCEC)

The bauhinia is the emblem of Hong Kong. The Golden Bauhinia Square, was a gift from the Central Government to mark the 1997 handover - an occasion that held tremendous significance for the world's largest nation and that stands out as a landmark event in 20th century history.

Ocean Park Hong Kong

Thrill rides, giant pandas and a world-class aquarium keep Ocean Park on Hong Kong's list of favourites. Opened in 1977, Ocean Park Hong Kong is a marine-life theme park featuring animal exhibits, thrill rides and shows. In 2012, its impressive ability to offer guests a world-class experience that blends entertainment with education and conservation was confirmed when it became the first Asian winner of the biannual Applause Award, the most prestigious award in the amusement and theme park industry.
