Dine and Drink

If you come to Hong Kong there are certain foods that you cannot leave without trying. From dim sum to fusion, put at least some of the must-eats of Asia's world city on your menu.

Dim Sum

Although a traditional style of dining, dim sum is in a constant state of evolution and there are always new and innovative dishes to taste. Here are some of the classics: Steamed shrimp dumpling, Shao mai, Pork bun and Cheung fen.

Seafood

It's fried, baked, steamed grilled, sliced and stuffed. More to the point, Hong Kong's seafood is very fresh. So fresh, you can see it swimming minutes before it's on your table. For a truly enjoyable seafood feast, desrt the downtown for a few hours and head for a seafood district.

Chinese Barbecue

Known as siu mei, resturants serving these barbecued meats - they also usually serve a delicious type of steamed chicken - are your window into traditional Chinese roasts. Including Barbecued pork, goose and roast pork.

Fusion

Try the fused flavours of Asia's world city. For a city that has been a crossroads of cultures since its inception, it is no wonder that Hong Kong's kitchens fuse the flavours of different countries and regions.

Fine Dining

Traditionally dominated by hotel restaurants - the city has over 30 five-star establishments - world-class cuisine and wine-pairing can now also be found all over the city, and often with breathtaking harbour views.

Late Night Eats

Some of the most popular night-time easts include Chiu Chow da lang, Chinese desserts hotpot, noodles and congee. Look for these, and you'll probably find a bustling nighthawk venue.
